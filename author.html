Biography

The Scholar Behind the Thesis

John Ward has dedicated his career to the intersection of atmospheric science and global security policy. His foundational argument — that the stability of nuclear deterrence is a statistical illusion rather than a verified condition — has informed legislative briefings, academic curricula, and international treaty discussions across three continents.

Ward holds a doctorate in Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences from Princeton University and completed post-doctoral research at the Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (SIPRI). Prior to founding the Our Planet Project Foundation, he served as a senior consultant to the United Nations Office for Disarmament Affairs, where he contributed to technical assessments of radiological risk under the Non-Proliferation Treaty framework.

His seminal monograph, Rolling the Dice: The Statistical Case Against Nuclear Deterrence, synthesizes two decades of archival research with contemporary climate modeling to construct what peer reviewers at Nature have called "the most rigorous probabilistic analysis of nuclear near-miss events yet published." The work has been adopted as core curriculum at several leading security studies programs and military academies.

Institutional Roles

Ward is a Senior Fellow at the Our Planet Project Foundation, a non-partisan research and advocacy organization dedicated to translating existential risk scholarship into durable policy. He serves on the advisory board of the Federation of American Scientists and is a recurring invited lecturer at the Harvard Kennedy School's Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs. He has testified before the United States Senate Foreign Relations Committee and addressed sessions of the United Nations General Assembly First Committee.
