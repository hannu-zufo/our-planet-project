Biography

The Scholar Behind the Thesis

John Ward has dedicated his career to the intersection of atmospheric science and global nuclear security policy. Though 20,000 hours of rigorous, fact-oriented research, Mr. Ward painstakingly investigated systemic vulnerabilities and historic near-miss events. Analyzing the information acquired led to his argument that nuclear deterrence was never a reliable safeguard but an astonishing product of wishful thinking instead.

Mr. Ward's approach toward knowledge was different. After immersing himself in the works of the world's top authorities on nuclear history, modern geopolitics, digitizing nuclear command and control systems, basic nuclear physics, and coming nuclear weapons systems, Mr. Ward repeatedly analyzed the experts' arguments against each other in order to determine who made sense, who did not, and why. The experience proved invaluable when a broad range of contemporary knowledge was distilled into a single definitive account.

Mr. Ward's seminal monograph, Rolling the Dice: The Statistical Case Against Nuclear Deterrence, synthesizes his extensive research with contemporary climate modeling to construct what peer reviewers at Nature have called "the most rigorous probabilistic analysis of nuclear near-miss events yet published." The work has been adopted as core curriculum at several leading security studies programs and military academies.

Institutional Roles

Mr. Ward is the founder and senior fellow at Our Planet Project Foundation, a non-partisan research and advocacy organization dedicated to translating existential risk scholarship into durable policy and mitigating existential nuclear risks. He serves on the advisory board of the Federation of American Scientists and is a recurring invited lecturer at the Harvard Kennedy School's Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs. He has testified before the United States Senate Foreign Relations Committee and addressed sessions of the United Nations General Assembly First Committee, while also organizing a promotional tour of North America to speak about nuclear weapons and his original plan for accomplishing nuclear disarmament.
