01 / The Mechanism

A Unique Approach Toward Legislative Leverage

Traditional lobbying efforts are designed to influence representatives who are often motivated primarily by money. Our strategy utilizes the "Direct Public Mandate" model to circumvent federal inertia by anchoring policy change at the municipal and state level, where civic participation is most direct and institutional resistance is lowest.

Phase I: Municipal Hardening

We work with local governments to pass "Existential Risk Assessment" ordinances. These laws require local municipalities to create detailed impact reports describing the effects of nuclear war on the local community. Those reports, combined with pressure from media coverage, can force the nuclear conversation onto agendas of those elected officials who are closest to their constituents.

Phase II: The Binding Ballot Initiative

In states that allow direct constitutional democracy, we provide the legal framework for ballot initiatives that mandate specific state-level safety protocols. Successful initiatives are not suggestions but binding legal instruments with the full force of government legislation.

Phase III: National Coordination

As the movement expands across key states, a favorable environment is created for introducing national legislation requiring the establishment of an international organization tasked with negotiating a universal nuclear disarmament treaty. Polling consistently reveals that over 75% of people worldwide support eliminating nuclear weapons – an untapped army of potential voters ripe for empowerment through organization.

02 / Implementation

The Scholar-Educator Pipeline

The Foundation's flagship publication ROLLING THE DICE WITH NUCLEAR WEAPONS: The Illusion of Control and the Path Forward is an indispensable tool for universities performing research, including military academies. By influencing the next generation of policy thinkers at the source, we hope that "Illusion of Control" becomes the new mantra for strategic thinking about military posturing and nuclear conflict.
