01 / The Core Argument

The Thesis of Systemic Fragility

For over seven decades, the architecture of global security has rested upon the doctrine of Rational Deterrence. This doctrine assumes by default that high-stakes systems are managed by perfect actors with access to infallible data. However, empirical analysis of the archival record reveals that our survival has frequently been the result of statistical fortune rather than systemic stability.

This project serves as an academic bridge between historical data, current fact, and future policy. One aspect involves synthesizing peer-reviewed atmospheric science regarding the impact of nuclear war on global climate. This integrated approach provides policymakers, educators, and researchers with the requisite framework for moving beyond political rhetoric into actionable strategy. Among the many factors involved, we argue that human fallibility is the greatest unpriced risk in modern nuclear geopolitics – an unpredictable variable which the "Rational Deterrence Theory" cannot begin to structurally accommodate.

02 / The Science

The Atmospheric Threshold

What value is deterrence if its failure plunges our planet into a nuclear winter where crops cannot grow? The threshold for global catastrophe is far lower than previously imagined by 20th-century strategists. Updated 3D aerosol models demonstrate that even a regional exchange could inject over 30 million tons of black carbon soot into the stratosphere, a volume sufficient to trigger a planetary-scale event that killed millions from starvation worldwide.

This contamination creates a self-reinforcing feedback loop that renders traditional agricultural cycles difficult or impossible for up to a decade. Modern computer climate models have enabled contemporary research to account for the "self-lofting" effect of black carbon soot, a powerful pollutant that absorbs heat from the sun's radiation before rising into the stratosphere where it blocks sunlight from reaching the Earth. The resulting decline in surface sunlight would trigger a significant reduction in global food production, with cascading humanitarian consequences that no nation or institution is positioned to manage.

03 / The Response

From Rhetoric to Strategy

Our Planet Project Foundation does not merely document risk — we propose a surgical legal intervention. No other plan is like it. Our "Direct Public Mandate" model utilizes a bottom-up legal architecture designed to bypass federal lobbyist inertia. By converting broad public consensus into legally binding municipal ordinances and state ballot initiatives, politicians can be forced to introduce meaningful legislation onto the national congressional calendar.

This is not advocacy. It is applied scholarship — a methodology for translating peer-reviewed findings into realistically enforceable policy. This site can only offer a limited amount of evidentiary basis for our work, however a definitive account compiled by nuclear scholar John Ward may be found in the Foundation's opus, ROLLING THE DICE WITH NUCLEAR WEAPONS: The Illusion of Control and the Path Forward.
