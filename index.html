01 / The Core Argument

The Thesis of Systemic Fragility

For over seven decades, the architecture of global security has rested upon the doctrine of Rational Deterrence. This doctrine assumes that high-stakes systems are managed by perfect actors with access to infallible data. However, empirical analysis of the archival record reveals that our survival has frequently been the result of statistical fortune rather than systemic stability.

This project serves as a scholarly bridge between historical data and future policy. By synthesizing peer-reviewed atmospheric science with the "Rolling the Dice" thesis, we provide policymakers, educators, and researchers with the framework necessary to move beyond political rhetoric and into actionable global strategy. The central argument is that the "Human Fallibility" variable is the most significant unpriced risk in modern geopolitics — a variable that Rational Deterrence theory structurally cannot accommodate.

02 / The Science

The Atmospheric Threshold

What value is deterrence if its failure plunges the planet into a nuclear winter in which crops cannot grow? The threshold for global catastrophe is far lower than previously imagined by 20th-century strategists. Updated 3D aerosol models demonstrate that even a regional exchange would inject an estimated 150 million tons of soot into the stratosphere — a volume sufficient to trigger planetary-scale agricultural collapse.

This injection creates a self-reinforcing feedback loop that renders traditional agricultural cycles impossible for up to a decade. Unlike earlier models, contemporary research accounts for the "self-lofting" effect: heated soot rises above the clouds, beyond the reach of precipitation, and remains suspended in the upper atmosphere for years. The resulting reduction in solar radiation would trigger a 70% collapse in global calorie production, with cascading humanitarian consequences that no nation or institution is positioned to manage.

03 / The Response

From Rhetoric to Strategy

The Our Planet Project Foundation does not merely document risk — we propose a surgical legal intervention. Our "Direct Mandate" model utilizes a bottom-up legal architecture designed to bypass federal lobbyist inertia. By converting broad-spectrum public consensus into legally mandated instruction through state-level ballot initiatives and municipal ordinances, we aim to force high-impact safety protocols onto the national legislative calendar.

This is not advocacy. It is applied scholarship — a methodology for translating peer-reviewed findings into durable, enforceable policy structures. The site presents the full evidentiary basis for that work, the strategy through which it is being implemented, and the scholarly record of the researcher behind it.
